Apartment Hunting Tool for UW Students

Problem Description

Overview of Topic

Looking for housing in Seattle is difficult, especially for college students. Most students live on campus, but after their 3rd year, almost many more students move out to live off-campus. While college students are looking for housing, they are not informed about how safe the neighborhoods are. College students must access this information to choose where to live wisely. This is a significant issue to discuss because there are some places in Seattle where it's not safe for college students to be living. We might also be looking to assist anybody looking for a place to live rather than only focusing on college students.

At the same time, with personal experience we have found that it is difficult to keep track of the list of apartments toured and there is not a designed website or application that can make a personalized pros and cons list. Making a personal comparision list is important for any decisions and choosing where to live is a big one.

Information Technology Problem

We are interested in making a website that will allow college students to look for an apartment/house around their college campus. Aside from looking for housing, our website offers to give students more information about the area they are interested in. We will be focusing on crime rate, sex offender rate, robbery rate, aggravated assault, etc. Well, anyone can google and search the crime rate in the area they are looking to move into, but it probably isn't the first thing that crosses their minds. This provided information gives the user the report they need to decide where to live.

Previous or Existing Solutions

There are many established websites for housing huntings and some of the more well-known ones include Apartments.com and Zillow. Both are similar in that they provide comprehensive information about the listings of apartments or houses. They usually include information like

  • pricing
  • floor plans
  • availability
  • amenities
  • additional fees

In our search of apartments around the University of Washington campus, both websites failed to mention specific amenities like study rooms that might be particularly helpful for students. Both Apartments.com and Zillow include features like location scores for walking and transit and also have a neighborhood map the users can interact with and explore. Apartments.com includes an addition interactive map for places of interest like restaurants, coffee shops, fitness, and shops. But the websites are not student eccentric and are lack of important information for commute like how long it takes to get to specific places on campus. Furthermore, they both do not have specific safety statistics like crime rates around the area, which is an important aspect when it comes to apartment hunting for students who often walks in the dark. Apartments.com does have a review section where users can leave reviews of specific apartment buildings but the reviews are mostly left by existing residents. Both Apartments.com and Zillow includes a "love" list where users can save interested houses or apartments, but they do not have functions where users can enter in their own notes about the apartment.

Proposed Application

Users

The users of the application include students and other individuals interested in apartments in a specific area. The user can be someone interested in keeping track of the apartments they see while apartment searching and may also use information from our website during their search. Users can range in age from students in college looking for housing to new working professionals looking for affordable housing.

Information Shown

The users can view information such as apartment name, location, amenities, number of bathrooms, number of bedrooms, safety rating, and more. They are able to input information as well to keep track of the apartments they are interested in or have seen. The file we will use will look similar to the file linked here.

User Interactions

  • The users will be able to organize and keep track of their searches by adding their searches and saving them. They will receive information on the availability, price and the location of the apartments. They will also have a section where they can input their own notes about the apartment during their hunting process and create a pros and cons list.
  • The users of this site will be able to search for available apartments in their desired residential areas. Since we want to focus on location safety, they will also be able to check the crime rates overall and against certain demographics (Race, gender, sexual orientation) in a certain area. Our site will have a bar graph that will show the percentage of crime in the specified city as compared to the national crime rate.

Why this App

Our website application will be a solution for University of Washington students who are looking for an apartment to live in. This solution has everything that current apartment hunting websites does and more with added statistics about location security and a section where users can input their own data. Our application is looking to ease the stress of apartment hunting and serves as an important tool for anyone looking for a place to live.

University of Washington students, faculty and staff receive alerts from the UWPD Police letting us know that a crime has occured. We receive these messages so regularly that it is alarming. Students living within the University District wish that they knew how dangerous the residential area is because maybe that would have deterred them from living there. Our website gives students or recent graduates the data they will need in order to choose a home that is both safe and affordable. It gives them access to everything they are looking for in a home.
